                            <!-- Definitions -->
                            <div class="bg-blue-50 p-4 rounded-lg border border-blue-200">
                                <h3 class="text-lg font-semibold mb-2">Key Concepts</h3>
                                <ul class="list-disc list-inside space-y-1">
                                    <li><strong>Inequality:</strong> The unequal distribution of income and/or wealth within a population.</li>
                                    <li><strong>Poverty:</strong>
                                        <ul class="list-none list-inside ml-4">
                                            <li><strong>Absolute:</strong> Lack of basic needs (e.g., food, shelter). Measured by the poverty line.</li>
                                            <li><strong>Relative:</strong> Income level below a certain proportion of the national average.</li>
                                        </ul>
                                    </li>
                                    <li><strong>Measurement Tools:</strong>
                                        <ul class="list-none list-inside ml-4">
                                            <li>- Lorenz Curve (visual representation)</li>
                                            <li>- Gini Coefficient (numerical index 0-1)</li>
                                        </ul>
                                    </li>
                                </ul>
                            </div>
                            
                            <!-- Key Statistics -->
                            <div class="bg-blue-50 p-4 rounded-lg border border-blue-200">

                                    <div class="text-center">
                                        <p class="text-3xl font-bold text-blue-800">~76%</p>
                                        <p class="text-sm">of global wealth is held by the richest 10% of the population.</p>
                                    </div>
                                    <hr>
                                    <div class="text-center">
                                        <p class="text-3xl font-bold text-blue-800">~700M</p>
                                        <p class="text-sm">people live in extreme poverty (less than $2.15/day).</p>
                                    </div>
                                </div>

                    <section>
                        <h2 class="text-2xl font-semibold text-blue-700 mb-4 border-b-2 border-blue-200 pb-2">
                            6.2. Explanatory Factors of Imbalances
                        </h2>
                        <p class="mb-4">Development imbalances are caused by a complex mix of internal and external factors, often reinforcing each other.</p>
                        <div class="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-4">
                            <div>
                                <h3 class="text-lg font-semibold mb-2">Internal Factors</h3>
                                <ul class="list-disc list-inside space-y-1">
                                    <li>Institutional quality (governance, corruption)</li>
                                    <li>Political instability</li>
                                    <li>Lack of human capital (education, health)</li>
                                    <li>Weak infrastructure</li>
                                </ul>
                            </div>
                            <div>
                                <h3 class="text-lg font-semibold mb-2">External Factors</h3>
                                <ul class="list-disc list-inside space-y-1">
                                    <li>Historical legacies (e.g., colonialism)</li>
                                    <li>Trade imbalances (e.g., terms of trade)</li>
                                    <li>External debt burden</li>
                                    <li>Vulnerability to global shocks</li>
                                </ul>
                            </div>
                        </div>
                    </section>

                            Link for ADE: Business & Development
                        </h3>
                        <p>For Business Administration students, it's crucial to understand how Multinational Enterprises (MNEs) and Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) impact development. While FDI can bring capital, technology, and jobs, its net effect depends on:</p>
                        <ul class="list-disc list-inside mt-2 ml-2">
                            <li><strong>Linkages:</strong> Creation of local supply chains vs. operating in "enclaves".</li>
                            <li><strong>Spillovers:</strong> Transfer of technology and managerial skills to local firms.</li>
                            <li><strong>"Bottom of the Pyramid" (BoP):</strong> Strategies that include the poorest populations as consumers and producers.</li>
                        </ul>
                    </section>

                        </h2>

                                <ul class="list-disc list-inside space-y-1">
                                    <li><strong>Bilateral Aid:</strong> Country-to-country (e.g., ODA - Official Development Assistance).</li>
                                    <li><strong>Multilateral Aid:</strong>
                                        <ul class="list-none list-inside ml-4">
                                            <li>- <strong>World Bank Group:</strong> Provides financing (IBRD, IDA) and technical assistance.</li>
                                            <li>- <strong>IMF:</strong> Focuses on macroeconomic stability.</li>
                                        </ul>
                                    </li>
                                    <li><strong>Non-Governmental Org. (NGOs):</strong> Often project-based and community-focused.</li>
                                </ul>
                            </div>
